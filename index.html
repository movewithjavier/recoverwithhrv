Heart Rate Variability: Your Body's Built-in Recovery Monitor

HRV is the most powerful biomarker for measuring recovery and readiness

Heart Rate Variability (HRV) is the variation in time between consecutive heartbeats. Unlike your heart rate, which tells you how many times your heart beats per minute, HRV reveals the subtle variations between each beat.

Higher HRV is generally associated with better health, improved performance, and greater resilience to stress. Lower HRV often indicates that your body is under stress from factors like poor sleep, overtraining, illness, or emotional strain.

By monitoring your HRV, you can gain valuable insights into your body's recovery status and make informed decisions about your training, nutrition, and lifestyle.
